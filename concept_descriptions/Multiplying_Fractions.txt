Multiplying fractions might seem complicated at first glance, but once you understand the basic principles, it becomes an easy task. This article will explain how to multiply fractions in detail.

Firstly, let's define what a fraction is. A fraction is a mathematical expression that represents the division of one quantity by another. It has two parts: the numerator, which is the number above the fraction bar, and the denominator, which is the number below the fraction bar.

Now, let's move on to the process of multiplying fractions. Unlike addition and subtraction of fractions, which require the fractions to have the same denominator, you can multiply fractions directly, regardless of their denominators.

Here are the steps for multiplying fractions:

1. Multiply the numerators: The first step in multiplying fractions is to multiply the numerators (the top numbers) of the fractions. This will give you the numerator of the answer.

2. Multiply the denominators: The next step is to multiply the denominators (the bottom numbers) of the fractions. This will give you the denominator of the answer.

3. Simplify the fraction: The final step is to simplify the fraction, if possible. This involves reducing the fraction to its lowest terms. To do this, you find the greatest common factor (GCF) of the numerator and the denominator, and then divide both the numerator and the denominator by the GCF.

1. Multiply the numerators: 2 x 3 = 6. So, the numerator of the answer is 6.

2. Multiply the denominators: 3 x 4 = 12. So, the denominator of the answer is 12.
